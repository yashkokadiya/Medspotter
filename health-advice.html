Cold

To alleviate cold symptoms, prioritize rest and hydration. Use saline nasal sprays or rinses to clear nasal passages, and humidify the air for comfort. For sore throats, gargle with salt water, sip warm liquids, and use lozenges or cough drops. Over-the-counter medications like pain relievers, decongestants, and cough suppressants can also provide relief. Remember to follow the instructions on medications and consult a doctor if symptoms worsen or persist.

Cough

Stay hydrated Drink plenty of fluids to thin mucus. Rest Your body needs energy to fight off the infection. Humidify the air This can soothe a dry cough. Over-the-counter medications Cough suppressants or expectorants may provide relief. Honey A spoonful of honey can help soothe a sore throat. Avoid irritants Stay away from smoke, dust, and other irritants.

Fever

For fever, prioritize rest, hydration, and symptom management. Get plenty of sleep to help your body fight the infection. Drink ample fluids, like water and clear broths, to prevent dehydration. Over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen can help alleviate discomfort. If fever persists, is accompanied by severe symptoms, or affects children, consult a healthcare professional.

Dengue

Dengue fever is a serious mosquito-borne illness that can cause severe symptoms. To prevent dengue, it's crucial to take preventive measures. Avoid mosquito bites by using mosquito repellent, wearing long sleeves and pants, and sleeping under a mosquito net. Additionally, eliminate stagnant water sources around your home, as these are breeding grounds for mosquitoes. If you suspect dengue, seek medical attention promptly.

Conjunctivitis

Conjunctivitis, often referred to as "pink eye," is a common eye infection caused by bacteria, viruses, or allergies. To manage conjunctivitis, it's essential to maintain good hygiene, avoid touching your eyes, and use over-the-counter eye drops or medications as directed by a healthcare professional. If symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical advice.
